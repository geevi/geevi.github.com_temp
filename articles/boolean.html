Generalized Harmonic Analysis over Abelian Group

Consider \(\mathbb{Z}_2 = \{0,1\}\), an abelian group under \(mod~2\) addition. We will be viewing this group as \(\{\pm 1\}\) under the operation of multiplication. These two are isomorphic by the following mapping \(0 \mapsto +1, 1 \mapsto -1\). The following is a well known theorem:

Theorem Any function of the form \(f:\{\pm 1\}^n \rightarrow\mathbb{R}\) can be written as a multilinear polynomial: \[ f(x) = \sum_{S \subseteq [n]} \hat{f}_S \prod_{i\in S}x_i \] This representation is unique.

Another way of saying this is that the functions \(\{\prod_{i\in S}x_i \}_{S\subseteq [n]}\) form an orthogonal basis for the \(2^n\) dimensional real vector space of functions from \(\{\pm 1\}^n\) to \(\mathbb{R}\). In this article we will be exploring the generalizations of this theorem to arbitrary finite (and some infinite) abelian groups.

It is well known by the structure theorem for finitely generated modules, that any finite abelian group is isomorphic to a direct sum of cyclic groups with prime order. Hence we will first analyze cyclic groups of prime order \(\mathbb{Z}_p\).

Harmonic analysis of \(\mathbb{Z}_p\)

Let

\(p\) be a prime
\(\mathbb{Z}_p\) be the cyclic group of order \(p\)
\(\Phi\) be the set of homomorphisms from \(\mathbb{Z}_p\) to \(\mathbb{C}\)

Claim : 1. \(\forall \chi \in \Phi, \chi(0)=1\)

  1. \(|\Phi| = p\)

  2. \(\Phi = \{ \chi_k(x) = \omega^{kx \mod p} \}\), where \(\omega\) is the \(p\)th root of unity.

Proof : Since \(\forall a \in \mathbb{Z}_p, a+0=a \Rightarrow \chi(a)\chi(0) = \chi(a)\) , then \(\chi(0)\) should be \(1\). Let \(1\) be a generator then \(b=k\times1 \Rightarrow \chi(b)=\chi(a)^k\)1. Hence \(\chi\) is completely determined by its value on the generator \(1\in \mathbb{Z}_p\). Since \(\chi(0)=\chi(p\times 1)=\chi(1)^p\), we have that \(\chi(1)\) must be one of the \(p\) roots of unity. Hence the last statement follows. QED
